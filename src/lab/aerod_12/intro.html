Hot Wire Anemometry




INTRODUCTION:


Hot-Wire Anemometry

It is an instrument for measuring air-speed using hot wires.


Operating principle

It is basically a thermal transducer.The principle of operation is:the heat transfer from a fine filament that is exposed to a cross flow varies with variation in the flow rate.The filament is made from a material that possesses a temperature coefficient f resistance,that is,if the temperature of the filament varies,so also does its resistance.The variation in resistance in resistance is monitored by various electronic methods that give signals relative to the variation in flow velocity or flow temperature.





Classification

Based on electronic instruments used,hot-wire anemometer can be classified into three categories:

  1. The constant temperature anemometer,which supplies the sensor with a heating current that varies with the fluid velocity in order to maintain constant sensor resistance and thus,constant temperature.
  2. Theconstant current anemometer,which supplies a constant heating current to the sensor.The variation in the sensor resistance with fluid flow velocity change causes voltage drop variation across the sensor.
  3. The pulsed wire anemometer,which measures velocity by momentarily heating a wire to heat the fluid around it.This spot of heated fluid is convected downstream to a second wire that acts as a temperature sensor; the time of flight of the hot spot is inversely proportional to fluid velocity.


Modes of operation

There are two modes of operation that are followed in hot-wire anemometry:
Constant current mode
The current flow through the hot wire is kept constant and variation in the wire resistance caused by the fluid flow are measured by monitoring the voltage drop variation across the filament.
Constant temperature mode
The hot-wire filament is placed in a feedback circuit that tends to maintain the hot wire at constant resistance and hence at a constant temperature. Fluctuations in the cooling of the filament are seen in variations n the current flow through the hot wire.


Mean Velocity Measurements in constant Current Hot-Wire Anemometer

The current is kept constant by connecting a large resistance Rsin series with heating circuit.The resistance Rs should be at least 1000 times larger than the wire resistance Rw.The wire resistance ratio is first set by setting R3. By adjusting Rs and observing galvanometer G,the bridge is brought to balance.This is done for different velocities and a static constant temperature calibration curve I2(mean) versus √U(mean) is plotted.
As the velocity increases Rwwill decrease.For each velocity the bridge has to be balanced and the value of Rwis noted and thus the flow velocity U is computed.



Turbulence level measurement with Hot-Wire Anemometer

The hot-wire anemometer can be used to measure free stream turbulence directly.It can be used for the measurement of turbulence at a very low speed also.It measures the instantaneous values of speed accurately and hence the turbulence defined as

Turbulence=Deviation from mean speed/Mean speed,


can be found directly.The time lag is practically negligible.The wire used is platinum or tungsten wire of about 0.015 mm diameter and about 10 mm long.The rate of heat dissipation H is given by

H=I2R=(A+B √V)(T-Ta)     King's formula


where I is the electric current through the wire,R is the wire resistance,A and B are constants to be found by the calibration experiments,V is the flow velocity,and T and Taare the wire and room temperatures,respectively.King's formula is the fundamental relation or hot-wire anemometry.


Limitations of Hot-Wire Anemometer

Even though the hot wire anemometer is considered to be a most successful instrument for measuring turbulence,it has many limitations,

  1. A non-linear character of heat transfer with respect to velocity and temperature.
  2. An onset of practical limitations due to the complex nature of the heat transfer between wire and fluid in compressible flow.
  3. A limitation set by the resolution power in space,that is,in the direction of the wire,due to its finite length.
  4. A limitation set by the resolution power in time,in the flow direction,due to the finite time constant of the hot wire.
