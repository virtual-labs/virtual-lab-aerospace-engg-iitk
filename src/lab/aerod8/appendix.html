Questions

  1. why strut type balance is preferable to wire type balance?

    Ans.Strut is preferable due to the following advantages over the wire type balance:

    1. The struts being rigid,their deflection can be kept at a very small value.
    2. The arranged enables the struts to be faired so that the tare and interference drag are minimized.
    3. By using a cross-spring pivot instead of knife-edge,ball bearings, and the like in the beam,the zero error can be minimized.
    4. By choosing proper linkages,interaction loads can be reduced to a small value.

    Major disadvantages of wire type balance:

    1. Large tare drag because of the exposed wires.It is difficult to streamline the wires and hence it is difficult to determine the magnitude of tare drag accurately.
    2. Bearings and linkages cause zero error
    3. Wires have the tendency to crystallize and break.
    4. Space occupied is very large.
  2. What are the advantages and disadvantages of mechanical balances compared to strain gauge type?

    Ans.Mechanical balances enjoys the following advantages over the strain gauges.

    1. Low installation cost.
    2. Lesser complexity and more simplicity.
    3. Interaction between the different load components are small as compared strain gauge.
    4. The balance and supporting structures have very high rigidity,so that the deflection of parts under the influence of maximum load is negligible.

    Disadvantages

    1. The strain gauge type balances are much more sensitive compared to the mechanical balances.
    2. The result obtained from the strain gauge type balances is more accurate then the mechanical balances.

  3. What are different types of wind tunnel balances?Compare their merits and demerits.

    Ans. Basically ,a wind tunnel balance is a device to measure the actual forces and moments acting on a model placed in the test-section stream. Based on the construction details,the wind tunnel balances are broadly clasified into:

    • Wire-type balance
    • Strut-type balance
    • Platform-type balance
    • Yoke-type balance
    • Strain gauge-type balance
