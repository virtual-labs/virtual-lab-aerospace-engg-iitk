Measure pressure distribution on a symmetric aerofoil at few angles of attack




INTRODUCTION:

A big part of aerodynamics problems deal with characterization of fluid flow around various kinds of bodies and we know fluid flow can be completely defined by pressure,temperature, density and velocity. Moreover density and velocity cannot be measured directly and are calculated indirectly through pressure measurements.Thus pressure and temperature are two variables which completely characterize the fluid flow.Since this experiment deals with subsonic flow hence temperature becomes constant and pressure is the only parameter which is most important.In this experiment a study of flow around a symmetric airfoil wing done by measuring pressure on its surface and then pressure coefficient and lift coefficient is calculated and plotted.
Experimental setup consist of a low speed tunnel,a symmetric airfoil wing with 32 pressure measuring fixed on its surface,manometer to measure pressure and another manometer which measures pressure head of free stream thus gives free stream velocity.Airfoil is chosen to be equal to width of tunnel so that wing can be treated to be of infinite length and flow could be roughly treated as two dimensional.


Pressure measuring devices:

The pressure measuring devices are meant for measuring pressure in fluid flow can be broadly divided into:

  1. Manometers
  2. Pressure Transducer


Manometer

A Manometer is a device to measure pressures.A common simple manometer consists of a U shaped tube of glass filled with some liquid.Typically the liquid is mercury because of its high density.There are various types of manometers used for measurement depending upon range of pressures to be measured and the degree of precession required.Some of the popular types of manometer are U-tube manometers,multitube manometers,and Bertz-type manometers.


Principle of Manometer

The basic measuring principle of the liquid manometer is that the pressure applied is balance by the weight of a liquid column.The sensitivity of the instrument depends on the density of manometric fluid used.Some of the commonly used manometric liquids are water,alcohol and mercury.
Since pressure in manometer is measured with respect to some reference pressure and the most common reference pressure is atmospheric pressure.


Twin Air or 5-D Tunnel

This tunnel is closed circuit type with two test sections:one is 3-D and other is 2-D.This powered by two 15 H.P fans and has variable speed control.The 2-D side of the tunnel has a contraction ratio of 12:1 and a 60" long working section with a cross section of 12" X 48".Maximum speed in this side is about 40 m/sec.On the 3-D side,the tunnel has a contraction of 11:1 and a working section of 24" X 36".Maximum speed in this side is about 30m/sec.


Pressure Coefficient

The pressure coefficient is a dimensionless number which describes the relative pressures throughout a flow field in fluid dynamics.The pressure coefficient is used in aerodynamics and hydrodynamics.Every point in a fluid flow field has its own unique pressure coefficient,Cp.
The pressure coefficient is very useful parameter for studying the flow of incompressible fluids such as water,and also the low speed flow of compressible fluids such as air.The relationship between the dimensionless coefficient and dimensional numbers is:



Cp=p-p∞/1/2ρ∞V2∞

Where,

p is the pressure at the point for which pressure coefficient is being evaluated
p∞ is the pressure in the free stream(i.e remote from any distrubance)
ρ∞ is the free stream fluid density
V∞ is the free stream velocity of the fluid,or the velocity of the body through the fluid.


Lift Coefficient

The lift coefficient is a dimensionless coefficient that relates the lift generated by an aerodynamic body such as a wing or complete aircraft,the dynamic pressure of the fluid flow around the body,and a reference area associated with the body.
Lift coefficient may be used to relate the total lift generated by an aircraft to the total area of the wing of the aircraft.In this application it is called theaircraft or platform lift coefficient CL
The lift coefficient CL is equal to

CL=L/1/2 ρ υ2A = 2L/ρ υ2A = L/qA

Where,

L is the lift force,
ρ is the fluid density
υ is the true airspeed
q is dynamic pressure,and
A is planform area.
The lift coefficient is dimensionless number.
