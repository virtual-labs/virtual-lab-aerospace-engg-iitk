Theoretical Proof of Hele-Shaw Analogy

The flow in the Hele-Shaw apparatus is actually a highly viscous flow between two parallel plates with a very small gap between them. In this flow,the inertia flow is negligible with respect to the viscous force.Under this condition the flow equation has the same form as taht of Euler's potential flow,however it does not satisfy the no-slip wall boundary condition.
