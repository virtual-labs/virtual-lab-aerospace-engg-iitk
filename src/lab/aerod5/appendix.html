Calibration of supersonic nozzle





Questions

  1. What are the methods for determining the test-section Mach number of a supersonic wind tunnel?

    Ans.The following methods may be employed for finding the test-section Mach number of supersonic wind tunnels:
    1. If Mach number is close to speed of sound to 1.6,then by measuring 'P' in test section and 'P0' in setting chamber & using P01/P=(1+(Y-1)/2 M2)Y/(Y-1)

    2. If M >1.6,we use the pitot pressure in test-section (P02) with total head in the settling chamber(P01) and normal shock relation.
    3.Measurement of static pressure P1 and pitot pressure P03 at the testsection axis & using Rayleigh pitot formula.

    4.Mach angle M from a Schlieren photograph of clean test section can be used since sin M=1/M1

  2. What is Mach wave and how it is different from shock waves?

    Ans. Mach wave is pressure wave traveling at the speed of sound caused by a slight change of pressure added to a compressible flow,whereas a shock wave is a kind of propogating disturbance across which there is a rapid change in pressure and temperature unlike a Mach wave.

  3. Explain Simple and non-simple region?

    Ans. The waves causing isentropic expansion & compression that are straight Mach lines,with constant flow condition and which obey vn =vn-1+(Qn-Qn-1)expansion & vn=vn-1-|Qn-Qn-1| compression are called simple regions.

    Non simple regions do not obey these conditions.

  4. Explain isentropic expansion.

    Ans. Any expansion that takes place adiabatically and reversibly is termed isentropic expansion.

  5. Explain why choking should take place only at the throat?

    Ans. The throat of a convergent divergent nozzle has the minimum area upto which flow remains subsonic and downstream of which it becomes supersonic.If choking occurs at any cross section other than minimum area then the geometry doesn't allow the transition to take place.

  6. What do you understand by under and over expansions?

    Ans. Under expansion is a condition of the nozzle delivery pressure to be higher than back pressure while over expansion occurs if nozzle delivery pressure is less than back pressure.

  7. Describe 'adapted nozzle'.Is it superior to over expanded nozzle?If so why?

    Ans.Adapted nozzle is one in which the nozzle delivery pressure is same as back pressure.It is better than over expanded nozzle for tesr purposes but as far as thrust generation in rocket is concerned under expanded is better.Over expanded nozzle have longer length and thus greater weight.
